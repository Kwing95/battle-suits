BATTLE SUITS (v1.06)

### PREMISE ###################################################################

Battle Suits is a card battle game played using a standard 52 deck of cards.
Players arrange card pairs into units, which are deployed and moved within a 4x5
playing field to gather resources and capture enemy units. Captures and
purchases add to the player's score pile, which is used to determine the winner
at the end of the game.

### GAME LAYOUT ###############################################################

####...      P1 Hand       # P1 Score Pile
2A 32 43 54  P1 Back Row
56 78 9J QK  P1 Front Row
#  #  #  #   Shop Row      # Deck  # Discard Pile
56 78 9J QK  P2 Front Row
2A 32 43 54  P2 Back Row
####...      P2 Hand       # P2 Score Pile

### SETUP #####################################################################

1 Shuffle the deck, then deal 14 cards to each player and deal 4 cards faceup in
  the middle of the field to be shop cards.
2 Both players populate their back row with 4 units. Before play begins, both
  cards in the back row are laid facedown so that placement is not influenced by
  any already-placed units. Once both players have filled their back rows, one
  card from each unit is flipped faceup.
3 The player that goes first is decided by a game of rock, paper, scissors.
4 Players take two Actions per turn until the Deck is empty.
5 Once the Deck is empty, for every 3 cards in a player's hand, 1 is added to
  their score pile (rounded down.) After this, the player with the most cards in
  their score pile is the winner.

### SUITS BEHAVIOR ############################################################

HEART: Allows a Unit to resist damage from an attacking Unit. (See Attack)

SPADE: Allows a Unit to attack an enemy Unit. (See Attack)

DIAMOND: Allows the player to purchase cards from the Shop. (See Purchase)

CLUB: Adds its own value to card it's paired with. For instance, a Unit made of
  5 CLUB and 5 SPADE behaves the same as 10 SPADE.

DOUBLES: Two cards of the same suit combine their values; for instance,
  8 DIAMOND 9 DIAMOND behave as a single unit with 17 DIAMOND power. Two CLUB
  cards have no special behavior.

JOKER: Allows a Unit to attack an enemy Unit in the back row. This discards the
  JOKER and sends its partner card back to the user's Hand. JOKER cards must
  always be faceup, even in the back row. Playing with JOKER cards is optional.
  A CLUB has no effect when paired with a JOKER.

### ACTIONS ###################################################################

DEPLOY: When the player has two or more cards in their Hand, they may form a
  Unit and place it in a vacant spot in the back row. [1]

MOVE: Move a Unit into an adjacent space. If that space is occupied, swap the
  position of those Units. [1]

REFACTOR: When two adjacent Units are in the back row, swap one card between
  them. Or, when one Unit is in the back row and at least one card is in
  the player's Hand, swap one card between the player's Hand and that Unit. [1]
  
  [1] VISIBILITY RULE: During any DEPLOY, MOVE, or REFACTOR Action, it must be
    ensured that both cards of any Unit in the front row are placed faceup, and
	Units in the back row must have one card faceup and one card facedown. The
	player may choose which card to show faceup during any Action that affects a
	card in the back row.
	  JOKER VISIBILITY: A JOKER card in the back row must always be faceup.

ATTACK: When one Unit is in the front row and has a SPADE, capture the opponent
  Unit in the front row directly across from it and send both cards to the
  attacker's Score Pile. [2]
    SPADE vs no HEART  The attacking Unit remains.
    SPADE = HEART      Both cards in the attacking Unit are discarded.
    SPADE < HEART      Attacking is not allowed.
    SPADE > HEART      The SPADE card is discarded and its partner card returns
	                   to the player's hand.
    DOUBLES: If both cards in the attacking Unit are necessary to defeat the
      target Unit (for instance, for [5 SPADE + 4 SPADE > 6 HEART] or [5 SPADE +
	  4 CLUB > 6 HEART], BOTH cards in the attacking Unit are discarded.)

PURCHASE: When a Unit is in the front row and has a DIAMOND of greater or equal
  value to the card in the Shop Row, the DIAMOND is spent and replaced with the
  card that was purchased. The vacancy in the Shop Row is immediately replaced
  with a card drawn from the Deck. [2]
    SINGLE CARD PURCHASES: If only one DIAMOND card is used for purchasing, the
	  DIAMOND card used is sent to the purchasing player's Score Pile.
    DOUBLE CARD PURCHASES: If both cards in the purchasing Unit are necessary to
      purchase the target card (for instance, [5 DIAMOND 4 DIAMOND > 6 SPADE] or
	  [5 DIAMOND 4 CLUB > 6 SPADE], BOTH cards in the purchasing Unit are
	  discarded.)
	CONSECUTIVE PURCHASES: So long as the purchasing card(s) are greater than or
	  equal to the combined value of the cards being purchased, the player may
	  continue purchasing the cards that are drawn to repopulate the Shop. For
	  instance, if 10 DIAMOND purchases 2 SPADE and the replacement cards are
	  3 HEART, 4 CLUB, 4 HEART, the player may purchase 2 SPADE, 3 HEART, and
	  4 CLUB because their combined value (9) is less than the value of
	  10 DIAMOND, but the remainder (1) is not enough to purchase 4 HEART.
    DIAMOND PURCHASES: Any DIAMOND card in the Shop Row at the beginning of an
	  action is free; subsequent DIAMOND cards purchased on the same turn are
	  charged against the value of the purchasing unit's DIAMOND value as
	  normal. Cards purchased for free go into the player's hand.
	JOKER PURCHASE: JOKER cards cost 10 DIAMOND to purchase.

  [2] ACES LOW RULE: For all card comparisons and arithmetic, ace and face
	cards are valued as follows: A = 1, J = 11, Q = 12, K = 13

DRAW: When a player has 7 or fewer cards across both rows and their Hand, they
  may draw two cards and add them to their Hand.
  
SACRIFICE: When a unit is in the front row, send both cards to the Discard pile
  and add the adjacent card in the Shop Row to the player's hand. If the
  sacrificing card is directly across from an enemy unit in the front row with a
  SPADE card, one card is sent to the Discard pile while the other is sent to
  the opponent's Score Pile.
  
PASS: A player may pass for one or both Actions on their turn.

REFRESH: If there are no DIAMOND cards in the Shop, the player may put all 4
  shop cards back into the Deck, shuffle it, and deal 4 new cards. This counts
  as 2 Actions.
